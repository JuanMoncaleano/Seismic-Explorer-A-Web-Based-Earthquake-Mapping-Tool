Earthquake Depth Information

Shallow Earthquakes (0-70 km deep) Intensity: Shallow earthquakes often produce stronger shaking near the epicenter compared to deep earthquakes of similar magnitude. Damage: They are generally more destructive in terms of structural damage, landslides, and tsunamis. Area Affected: The area of strong shaking is usually more localized.

Intermediate-depth Earthquakes (70-300 km deep) Intensity: The shaking is generally less intense near the surface than it is for shallow earthquakes. Damage: They usually cause less damage to structures near the surface. Area Affected: The area of strong shaking can be more widespread, affecting regions farther from the epicenter.

Deep Earthquakes (300-700 km deep) Intensity: These earthquakes typically produce the least amount of shaking near the Earth's surface. Damage: They are the least likely to cause significant damage or tsunamis. Area Affected: The shaking can be felt over a large area, but it's generally not strong enough to cause damage.
